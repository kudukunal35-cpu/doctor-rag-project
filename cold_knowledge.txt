Medical Topic: Cold
Overview: Common cold is a mild viral infection affecting nose and throat.
Common Symptoms: Sneezing, sore throat, runny nose, mild fever, cough, congestion, tiredness.
What To Do: Rest, drink warm fluids, maintain hydration, use steam inhalation, eat light food, monitor symptoms.
What Not To Do: Do not take unnecessary antibiotics, do not ignore worsening breathing issues, do not stay dehydrated.
Precautions: Wash hands, avoid close contact with sick people, cover mouth while coughing, maintain immunity through good diet and rest.
Doctor Focus: Distinguish viral cold from serious infection, monitor breathing, and manage supportive care.
Quick Summary: Common cold is usually mild and self-limiting, but worsening symptoms need evaluation.

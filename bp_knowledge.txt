Medical Topic: Blood Pressure
Overview: High blood pressure is a condition where force of blood against artery walls remains elevated, increasing heart and stroke risk.
Common Symptoms: Often no symptoms, but some patients may experience headache, dizziness, chest discomfort, or blurred vision.
What To Do: Reduce salt intake, take medicines regularly, check blood pressure often, exercise, manage stress, sleep properly.
What Not To Do: Do not miss medication, do not eat excess salty food, do not smoke, do not ignore repeated high readings.
Precautions: Maintain healthy weight, reduce alcohol, avoid smoking, follow low-salt diet, monitor BP regularly.
Doctor Focus: Review blood pressure trends, adherence, cardiovascular symptoms, medication response, and complication risk.
Quick Summary: Blood pressure control depends on lifestyle, medication adherence, regular monitoring, and complication prevention.

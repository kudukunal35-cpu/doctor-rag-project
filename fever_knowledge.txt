Medical Topic: Fever
Overview: Fever is a temporary increase in body temperature, usually due to infection or inflammation.
Common Symptoms: High temperature, chills, sweating, headache, weakness, body pain, dehydration.
What To Do: Take rest, drink fluids, monitor temperature, eat light food, use doctor-advised fever medicine.
What Not To Do: Do not ignore dehydration, do not overuse antibiotics, do not eat heavy oily food, do not ignore very high fever.
Precautions: Stay hydrated, maintain hygiene, wash hands, avoid infected people, monitor symptoms carefully.
Doctor Focus: Check cause of fever, monitor temperature trends, identify danger signs, rule out infection-related complications.
Quick Summary: Fever usually indicates infection. Mild cases improve with rest, fluids, and monitoring, but severe symptoms require medical review.
